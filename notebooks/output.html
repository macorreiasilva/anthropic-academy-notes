# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete aiming to complete marathon training with optimal carbohydrate loading:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3500 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (130g), Fat (95g), Carbs (480g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (6:30 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), sunflower seeds (20g), and honey (20g). Low-fat milk (240ml).
  * Protein: 15g, Fat: 8g, Carbs: 95g

* **Pre-Run Snack (8:00 AM):** White bread (80g) with hummus (40g) and tomato slices (50g). Orange juice (250ml).
  * Protein: 8g, Fat: 5g, Carbs: 70g

* **Post-Run Recovery Meal (10:30 AM):** Vegetarian pasta with marinara sauce (250g cooked pasta, 150ml sauce), lentils (100g cooked), and olive oil (15ml). Side salad with mixed greens (100g) and balsamic vinegar (15ml).
  * Protein: 35g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 120g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Brown rice (150g cooked) with chickpea curry (200g chickpeas cooked in sauce with vegetables: onion 80g, bell pepper 70g, carrot 60g, coconut milk 100ml). Whole wheat bread (60g).
  * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Afternoon Snack (4:00 PM):** Rice cakes (50g) with peanut butter alternative (tahini, 30g) and jam (20g). Dried mango (80g).
  * Protein: 10g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 85g

* **Dinner (7:00 PM):** Baked tofu (180g) with sweet potato (250g), steamed broccoli (150g), and olive oil drizzle (10ml). Quinoa (80g cooked).
  * Protein: 26g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 95g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Greek yogurt (200g, low-fat) with granola (40g) and blueberries (80g).
  * Protein: 18g, Fat: 5g, Carbs: 45g

This meal plan prioritizes complex carbohydrates and plant-based proteins to fuel endurance performance while respecting vegetarian and nut-free restrictions. Strategic timing around training maximizes glycogen storage and recovery.
